Research

When creating the world of La Cage aux Folles, understanding the history of Southern France, the development of burlesque (Ziegfeld's Follies), the layout and style of French Riviera gay clubs and helicopter clubbing culture, Southern French port construction, French apartment interior design from multiple periods, popular color schemes, cultural and social gathering locales, and atmospheric accoutrements (fashion, music, food, cultural accessories, socio-economic signifiers, etc.)

Sketching: Blocking (Functionality)

After documenting all of the necessary design elements from the Pentacost script, blocking through sketching tracks the movements and actions of each actor/actress from a top-down view. This establishes the minimun necessary distance between objects and aids in audience-set angles of view. Blocking also maximizes return on actor movement and increases visibilty and stronger angles/stage positioning.

Sketching: Storyboarding and Perspective (Use Case)

Storyboarding defines the powerful emotional and storytelling moments of the action, ensuring that the designs not only work with, but maximize those peaks.
Perspective sketching translates the lose, aerial view blocking and the conceptual storyboarding sketches into definative locations with precise sizes.

Drafting: Detail and Aesthetics

A continuation of the detail work begun with perspective sketching, drafting draws upon the extensive research to fully render each scenic element as part of the world being created. Done in to exact scale, the focus of drafting is to polish each little piece in a construction-ready blueprint.

Modeling: Proofing Scale and Construction

Modeling is the three-dimensional culmination of drafting. Each design element is rendered to scale to triple check spacing, aesthetics, and feasability in full-scale.
