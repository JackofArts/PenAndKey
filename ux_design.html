Europe 1477

An Overview

Designed for players with a passion for history and creative storytelling, each player has control of a nation which he or she must guide through the ages with storytelling, creativity, and strategy.

The Project

Similar games exist but are run though, and structured by, modifications to existing video games or in a forum setting. This often leads to the games struggling to attract players and ending prematurely. The goal of Europe1477 was to create a codified set of rules and algorithms to decrease the workload on moderators while providing a stable and friendly platform with the possiblity of expansion to allow custom-created games.

Users Groups

Students:

Students' needs would focus on the game providing a more interactive and enjoyable medium of education. There would be a large varience in level of student interest in such a game and therefore the game should be user friendly and reward players who are more invested without punishing students playing only as a school requirement. Gamers:

Many gamers, history enthusiasts, and storytellers already use similar games through verious media (such as: game forums or reddit). These users would be most interested in Europe 1477 providing a dedicated platform. Many of these users are vetern players and moderators and would be interested in tailoring individual games to their personal tastes. Desired Features: b, c, d, h, i, k

Designing Europe 1477

    Designing the game:

  • Testing of similar existing game concepts
  • Hundreds of hours of research on the political, historical, socio-economic, religious, industrial, military, and cultural climate of late Medeival and Renaissance Europe
  • Writing and testing algorithms to strike the balance between realism and playability
  • Allowing player freedom of choice to shape the game
  • Creating structures to reduce moderator workload without limiting players
  • Compiling statistics for each nation and constructing the map
  • ALPHA test
  • Refining gameplay

    Designing the game platform:

  • BETA test for player painpoints
  • Sketch layout designs for a platform that encourages communication and player creation
  • Analyize and modify designs to ensure platform is robust enough for players who are more involved while not penalizing more casual players
  • Identify basic components: player interface, contacts and chat features, game searching and hosting, templature and publication/update pushpin system
  • Prototype platform with Photoshop and InvisionApp
  • Identify secondary components for prototype expansion: moderator calculators and interface, ability to customize starting national statistics and map, ability to customize templates available to players
